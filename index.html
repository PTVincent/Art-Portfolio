About

The Artist Behind the Ink
✦ Anno MMXXVI ✦

Hello

I'm Parker — a digital illustrator and self-taught concept artist working out of the Virginia coast. My work lives in the darker corners of fantasy: hooded warlocks and half-forgotten gods, crocodilian warlords and crystal-veined caverns, giants of crustacean and armor alike. I build worlds in ink and then write about how they ended.

I illustrate for tabletop players, dungeon masters, streamers, and fellow world-builders. Each commission is a collaboration — your character, your creature, your villain, rendered with the weight and menace it deserves. I work in painted busts, half-bodies, full illustrations, and creature design sheets, with a particular fondness for villains and the things that lurk beyond the firelight.

This site is a portfolio of my original work and the stories behind it. Every illustration here has a tale attached — open the journal and read.

Commissions

What I Offer
I

Character Portraits

Busts, half-bodies, and full illustrations of your heroes and villains. Painted with care, built from your brief, rendered in the dark-fantasy register — warlocks, tieflings, revenants, or whatever stalks your campaign.

II

Creature & Villain Design

Homebrew monsters, BBEGs, eldritch patrons, and the unnamed things at the end of the dungeon. Full design sheets available — anatomy, silhouette, color logic. A specialty of mine.

III

Worldbuilding Pieces

Environments, scenes, banners, key art for your homebrew setting or original IP. If you're building a world and need it shown, this is where we collaborate.

How It Works

From your brief to the final piece
01

Reach Out

Email me with your idea — character backstory, reference images, mood, anything you have. Don't worry if it's rough; the conversation is part of the process.

02

Quote & Sketch

I'll send a quote based on scope and complexity. Once 50% is paid, you'll get a rough sketch within a few days for your approval before I commit to color.

03

Paint & Deliver

I refine the piece with revisions baked in along the way. Final delivery is typically one to two weeks from sketch approval, sent in full resolution.
